The search tree of branch and bound is a subset of that of forward search


because branch and bound can ignore portions it knows are not optimal.


Due to the upper bound, branch and bound evaluates moving right first,


and because that happens to be optimal, it is able to immediately identify the


optimal sequence of actions and avoid exploring other actions. If the start


and goal states were reversed, the search tree would be larger. In the worst


case, it can be as large as forward search.
